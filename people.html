Our People

Our laboratory brings together researchers from molecular biology, physiology, bioinformatics, and clinical medicine, united by a shared interest in the mechanisms of metabolic disease.

Professor Antonio (Toni) Vidal-Puig
Principal Investigator · Professor of Molecular Nutrition & Metabolism · Fellow, Academy of Medical Sciences
⌄

As a Professor of Molecular Nutrition and Metabolism at Cambridge University, Professor Vidal-Puig leads research as Principal Investigator at the Medical Research Council Metabolic Diseases Unit (MRC MDU) and the Wellcome Sanger Institute. He also serves as Scientific Director of the Cambridge Phenomics Center, specializing in advanced murine metabolic phenotyping, and as Honorary Consultant in Metabolic Medicine at Addenbrooke's Hospital.

The TVPLab explores the molecular mechanisms linking obesity to insulin resistance, diabetes, and cardiometabolic complications, aiming to identify new therapeutic pathways. He trained in medicine in Valencia, Spain, and completed postdoctoral research at Harvard Medical School before joining Cambridge. He is a Fellow of the Academy of Medical Sciences, holds an ERC Advanced Grant, and has received the Society of Endocrinology Medal and the Hippocrates International Prize for Nutrition Research.

Dr. Rodrigo Santos

Dr Rodrigo Santos is a Visiting Entrepreneur at the TVP Lab with the goal to identify commercial and translation opportunities within the group. He has deep expertise in cell biology and the development and application of innovative cell technologies across biotech and pharma.

Rodrigo is an Entrepreneur in Residence (EiR) at Cambridge Innovation Capital (CIC). Prior to joining CIC as an EiR in 2026, Rodrigo was CTO of clock.bio. His biotech experience spans roles including EiR at Novo Holdings, Director of Cell Technologies at Mogrify, Head of Technology at bit.bio, and Principal Scientist at Horizon Discovery.

He actively contributes to the scientific community through multiple Scientific Advisory Boards and entrepreneurship mentoring programmes. Rodrigo completed his PhD at the Stem Cell Institute, University of Cambridge.
