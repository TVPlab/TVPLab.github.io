Our Research

Our laboratory investigates the molecular and cellular mechanisms that govern adipose tissue biology. We focus on white adipose tissue expandability — the capacity of fat depots to safely accommodate surplus energy — and the consequences when this capacity is exceeded, leading to ectopic lipid deposition and metabolic disease.

We also explore lipotoxicity in peripheral organs, the thermogenic potential of brown adipose tissue and skeletal muscle, and the immunometabolic role of adipose-resident macrophages in driving chronic inflammation and systemic insulin resistance.
