Postdoctoral Research Associate — Brown Adipose Tissue Thermogenesis

Postdoctoral Full-time 3 Years Open

We seek a highly motivated postdoctoral researcher to join our thermogenesis programme, funded by a prestigious ERC Advanced Grant. The successful candidate will use mouse models, primary cell culture, and multi-omics approaches to investigate the molecular control of adaptive thermogenesis in brown adipose tissue and skeletal muscle. A background in adipose biology, metabolism, or cell biology is essential; experience with mouse genetics and transcriptomics is highly desirable.

Postdoctoral Research Associate — Adipose Tissue Immunology & Fibrosis

Postdoctoral Full-time 2 Years Open

This position offers an exciting opportunity to investigate macrophage-driven fibroinflammation in obese adipose tissue. The postholder will integrate flow cytometry, single-cell sequencing, and in vivo mouse studies to dissect the pathways linking chronic inflammation to adipose fibrosis and systemic insulin resistance. Experience in immunology or cell biology is essential.

PhD Opportunities

PhD Studentship — White Adipose Tissue Expandability and Lipotoxicity

PhD Fully Funded 4 Years Open

A fully-funded 4-year PhD studentship is available through the MRC Metabolic Diseases Unit DTP programme. The project will use cutting-edge mouse genetics, adipocyte biology, and multi-omics approaches to investigate the mechanisms controlling WAT expansion and the consequences of expandability failure for peripheral organ lipotoxicity. Candidates must hold (or expect) a first or upper-second class degree in a relevant biomedical science discipline.

PhD Studentship — Single-Cell Genomics of Adipose Macrophages

PhD Fully Funded 4 Years Open

In collaboration with the Wellcome Sanger Institute, this studentship will use single-cell and spatial transcriptomics to dissect macrophage heterogeneity in obese adipose tissue. The student will gain expertise in single-cell sequencing, bioinformatics, and in vivo mouse models. A strong interest in immunology or computational biology is desirable.
