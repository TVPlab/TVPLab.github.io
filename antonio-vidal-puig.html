Professor Antonio (Toni) Vidal-Puig

Principal Investigator · Professor of Molecular Nutrition & Metabolism · Fellow, Academy of Medical Sciences

Biography

As Professor of Molecular Nutrition and Metabolism at Cambridge University, Professor Vidal-Puig leads research as Principal Investigator at the Medical Research Council Metabolic Diseases Unit (MRC MDU) and the Wellcome Sanger Institute. He also serves as Scientific Director of the Cambridge Phenomics Center, specialising in advanced murine metabolic phenotyping, and as Honorary Consultant in Metabolic Medicine at Addenbrooke's Hospital.

The TVPLab explores the molecular mechanisms linking obesity to insulin resistance, diabetes, and cardiometabolic complications, aiming to identify new therapeutic pathways. He trained in medicine in Valencia, Spain, and completed postdoctoral research at Harvard Medical School before joining Cambridge.

He is a Fellow of the Academy of Medical Sciences (FMedSci), Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians (FRCP), holds an ERC Advanced Grant, and has received the Society of Endocrinology Medal and the Hippocrates International Prize for Nutrition Research. In 2025 he was elected to the Academia Europaea.
