About Us

Professor Antonio Vidal-Puig

Professor Antonio Vidal-Puig is a Professor of Molecular Nutrition and Metabolism at the University of Cambridge and a Wellcome Trust Senior Investigator. He leads the TVP Lab at the Institute of Metabolic Science, Addenbrooke's Hospital, Cambridge.

Professor Vidal-Puig received his MD and PhD from the University of Valencia, Spain, and completed postdoctoral training at Harvard Medical School. He joined Cambridge in 1999 and has since established an internationally recognised programme in adipose biology and metabolic disease.

He has published more than 300 peer-reviewed papers and has been recognised with numerous awards, including the Rank Prize in Nutrition and the Novo Nordisk Prize for outstanding contributions to diabetes and metabolic research.

Our Mission

The TVP Lab is dedicated to understanding the fundamental molecular and cellular mechanisms that govern how the body stores and expends energy. We believe that a deep mechanistic understanding of adipose tissue biology is essential to developing effective treatments for obesity, type 2 diabetes, and related cardiometabolic diseases.

Our interdisciplinary team spans molecular biologists, physiologists, bioinformaticians, and clinician-scientists, working together to translate laboratory discoveries into insights with real clinical relevance.

We are proud to be part of the University of Cambridge's Institute of Metabolic Science, one of Europe's foremost centres for metabolic disease research, embedded within Addenbrooke's Hospital — a world-class NHS teaching hospital.
