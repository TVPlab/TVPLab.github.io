About

The TVP Lab investigates the molecular and cellular mechanisms governing adipose tissue biology, energy metabolism, and metabolic disease at the Institute of Metabolic Science, University of Cambridge.

Principal Investigator

Professor Antonio Vidal-Puig

Professor of Molecular Nutrition & Metabolism · Wellcome Trust Senior Investigator · Fellow, Academy of Medical Sciences

Professor Antonio Vidal-Puig is Professor of Molecular Nutrition and Metabolism at the University of Cambridge and a Wellcome Trust Senior Investigator. He leads the TVP Lab at the Institute of Metabolic Science, Addenbrooke's Hospital, Cambridge, and serves as Director of the Cardiometabolic Programme at the Heart & Lung Research Institute.

Professor Vidal-Puig received his MD and PhD from the University of Valencia, Spain, and completed postdoctoral training at Harvard Medical School. He joined Cambridge in 1999 and has since established an internationally recognised programme in adipose biology and metabolic disease.

He has published more than 300 peer-reviewed papers and has been recognised with numerous awards, including the Rank Prize in Nutrition, the Novo Nordisk Prize for outstanding contributions to diabetes and metabolic research, and the Society of Endocrinology Medal.

Our Mission

Understanding Energy Metabolism at the Molecular Level

The TVP Lab is dedicated to understanding the fundamental molecular and cellular mechanisms that govern how the body stores and expends energy. We believe that a deep mechanistic understanding of adipose tissue biology is essential to developing effective treatments for obesity, type 2 diabetes, and related cardiometabolic diseases.

Our interdisciplinary team spans molecular biologists, physiologists, bioinformaticians, and clinician-scientists, working together to translate laboratory discoveries into insights with real clinical relevance.

We are proud to be part of the University of Cambridge's Institute of Metabolic Science — one of Europe's foremost centres for metabolic disease research, embedded within Addenbrooke's Hospital, a world-class NHS teaching hospital.

Location & Facilities

Institute of Metabolic Science, Cambridge

We are based at the MRC Metabolic Diseases Unit within the Institute of Metabolic Science, Addenbrooke's Hospital. The IMS brings together clinical and basic science researchers to advance understanding and treatment of metabolic and endocrine diseases.

Our laboratory has access to state-of-the-art imaging, genomics, and metabolomics platforms, and maintains close links with the Wellcome Sanger Institute, the MRC Epidemiology Unit, and clinical departments across the Cambridge Biomedical Campus.
