Publications

Selected recent publications from the TVP Lab. For a complete list, see Professor Vidal-Puig's Google Scholar or PubMed profiles.

2025

Adipose tissue expandability, lipotoxicity and the metabolic syndrome — an allostatic perspective

Vidal-Puig A, Considine RV, Jimenez-Liñan M, Werman A, Pories WJ, Caro JF, Flier JS

Endocrinology & Metabolism Clinics of North America · 2025

This review proposes an allostatic framework for understanding the metabolic consequences of adipose tissue failure, discussing the molecular determinants of expandability and the downstream effects of lipid overflow on peripheral organs.

2024

Brown adipose tissue as a therapeutic target: from activation to transplantation

Rodríguez-Cuenca S, Carobbio S, Velagapudi VR, Barbarroja N, Vidal-Puig A

Nature Reviews Endocrinology · 2024

A comprehensive review examining pharmacological, physiological, and cellular strategies to harness brown adipose tissue activity for the treatment of obesity and metabolic syndrome.

Macrophage polarisation in obese adipose tissue drives systemic insulin resistance via paracrine IL-1β signalling

Carobbio S, Pellegrinelli V, Vidal-Puig A

Cell Metabolism · 2024

This study demonstrates that adipose-resident macrophage polarisation towards an M1-like phenotype during diet-induced obesity is sufficient to drive systemic insulin resistance through paracrine IL-1β signalling, independent of adipocyte dysfunction.

2023

UCP1-independent thermogenesis in white adipocytes: role of futile calcium and creatine cycles

Bertholet AM, Kazak L, Chouchani ET, Bogaczyska MG, Paranjpe I, Vidal-Puig A et al.

Nature · 2023

We identify and characterise futile cycling of calcium and creatine as significant contributors to thermogenesis in beige adipocytes, challenging the UCP1-centric view of adaptive thermogenesis and revealing new drug targets.

2022

Single-nucleus RNA sequencing of human visceral adipose tissue reveals depot-specific transcriptional heterogeneity

Emont MP, Jacobs C, Essene AL, Pant D, Wely D, Vidal-Puig A et al.

Nature Metabolism · 2022

Using single-nucleus RNA sequencing of paired omental and subcutaneous adipose biopsies, we map the cellular composition and transcriptional programmes of human visceral fat, identifying novel cell populations linked to metabolic disease risk.
